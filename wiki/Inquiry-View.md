# Inquiry View

The Inquiry View is the second major workspace in Radial Timeline. It is designed for corpus-level manuscript analysis rather than scene-by-scene editing.

**Open**: Command palette → **`Radial Timeline: Open Inquiry`**, or click the waves icon in the ribbon.

## Overview

Use Inquiry View when you want to step back and evaluate how scenes, books, and supporting material work together as a system.

The view centers on several distinct UI surfaces:

*   **Inquiry glyph** — the main radial analysis display
*   **Minimap** — scene and corpus context tied to findings
*   **Corpus Manager** — in-view scope and corpus overrides
*   **Briefing Manager** — recent sessions, reopening, reset, and purge actions
*   **AI Engine Popover** — engine readiness, request size, and AI configuration status

## What Inquiry View Is For

Use Inquiry View when you want to:

*   scan a book or saga for loose ends, continuity issues, and pressure gaps
*   compare Flow and Depth across zones
*   limit or override the current corpus without leaving the view
*   generate briefings and briefing articles from current findings
*   write inquiry action notes back to scenes
